Purpose

The UX Design/Prototype stage is important because it helps turn ideas into something you can see and test before building the actual product. It allows designers to figure out how users will use the product and fix any problems early on. This stage also helps gather feedback to make improvements and makes sure the product is easy to use and meets business needs. By testing designs early, it saves time and money, and helps everyone on the team—designers, developers, and clients—understand the plan better.

Methods/ Activities

  • Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Typically the starting point for designing an application, these allow for quick iterations on layout and structure, helping to refine ideas early in the design process based on client feedback without the distraction of visual details.
    Tools: Balsamiq, Mockflow, Mockframe, Wireframe.cc, Figma, AdobeXD, Sketch
  • High-Fidelity Mockups / Prototypes: These provide a detailed representation of the final product, incorporating a design system and style guide, which helps stakeholders visualize the end result and ensures alignment before development.
    Tools: Figma, AdobeXD, Sketch

Design System

  • Creating or following design systems and style guides is crucial for a project because they ensure consistent design across all platforms. This consistency speeds up the design process and helps teams work together more effectively. In UX design prototypes, a design system provides clear guidelines for elements and interactions, allowing designers to focus on user needs. This results in a more cohesive and efficient product that users will appreciate.
  • Example: GOV.UK Design System

Design Principles to consider

  • User-centricity and user control: Prioritize the needs and preferences of users to empower them in their interactions.
  • Consistency: Maintain uniformity in design elements and behavior to enhance usability and familiarity.
  • Information Architecture: Consider the overall structure, navigation, and layout of the design to facilitate intuitive user journeys.
  • Context: Design with the user's environment and situation in mind to create relevant and meaningful experiences.
  • Accessibility: Apply basic accessibility principles, such as color contrast, text size, and font readability, to ensure inclusivity for all users.
