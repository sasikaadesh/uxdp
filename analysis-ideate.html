Analysis and Ideate

  1. Home
  2. Analysis and Ideate

Purpose

  • Turn Research Into Design Ideas: Take insights from user research and use them to come up with ideas that can be turned into actual design solutions.
  • Identify and Prioritize Problems and Needs: Clearly define the main challenges and requirements based on research, then decide which ones are most important to solve first.

Methods/ Activities

  • Personas: Create user personas based on research. These personas represent the main groups of users and help us understand their goals, behaviors, needs, and challenges. They guide our design by keeping it focused on real users.
    Ex: User Personas
  • User Flows & Journey Maps:
    • High-Level User Flow: Map out the big picture of how users will move through the product or service from start to finish. This shows the main steps users will take to complete key tasks.
      Ex: User Flows
      • Sub-User Flows: Break down more complex actions into smaller steps or tasks. This helps us see the details of how users will interact with the product.
    • User Journey Map: Show a detailed visual of the user's experience from start to finish, including all their interactions, emotions, challenges, and opportunities to improve their experience.
  • Paper Wireframing: Draw simple wireframes on paper to sketch out ideas quickly. This helps us explore design options without investing too much time or effort in detailed designs early on.
    Ex: Paper Wireframes
    • Card Sorting & Affinity Diagrams:
    • Card Sorting: Ask users or team members to sort pieces of content or features into groups to see how they naturally organize information. This helps us figure out the best way to structure content or navigation. Card sorting is very valuable, particularly for determining information architecture, because it reveals how certain items would be grouped together.
    • Affinity Diagrams: Collect ideas, insights, or user feedback and organize them into groups or themes to spot patterns, problems, or opportunities that will help in designing the solution.
      Ex: Card Sorting
